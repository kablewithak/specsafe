# V2 Rejected Scenario-Family and Case Ideas

This ledger records rejected proposal ideas before any V2 fixture byte or label exists. It prevents
silent drift toward duplicate, ambiguous, or outcome-shaped evidence.

| Rejected idea | Rejection reason | Boundary preserved |
|---|---|---|
| A final family mirroring a previous closed experiment's case structure. | It risks rebuilding a consumed historical pattern rather than creating independently authored held-out evidence. | V1 evidence quarantine. |
| A calibration family that selects its workload mix after labels are written. | The selection would become outcome-dependent authoring. | Causal fixture authoring. |
| A confidence-bucket family designed around exact desired metric movement. | It would encode evaluation goals into fixture construction. | Honest held-out assessment. |
| A final family with a source template derived from a calibration family. | It would undermine source-template independence. | Cross-split lineage isolation. |
| A context-specific family intended to justify separate model parameters. | The selected candidate is global-only and has no approved subgroup terms. | Candidate-method contract. |
| A case whose status can be inferred from calibration balancing. | It risks making final-evaluation composition predictable before assessment. | Final-evaluation quarantine. |
| A family requiring a remote corpus, provider state, or opaque external model. | V2 is local-first synthetic evidence with inspectable provenance. | Reproducibility and privacy posture. |
| A case that needs a policy action or utility label to be meaningful. | Calibration evidence precedes policy and utility research. | Phase ordering. |
| A proposal that stores token IDs, confidence values, prompts, or labels. | This phase authorizes identifiers and lineage only, not fixture content. | Pre-fixture governance boundary. |

## Rejection rule

A future idea must be rejected unless it has a distinct diagnostic failure mode, a lawful split
assignment, a non-reused source-template fingerprint, and no dependence on known V2 or V1 outcomes.
